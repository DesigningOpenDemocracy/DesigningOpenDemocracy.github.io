If you've visited DOD's Philosophy page before, it just changed shape. The full accountability framework that used to live there — the rigorous, AI-reviewed standard for what belongs in the [Democracy Landscape](../../organisations/organisations.md) — has moved. The page itself is now something different: an open space for DOD members to argue about democracy.

## What moved, and where

The framework, its [Soul Document](../../projects/accountability-framework/soul.md), and the record of dialogue between six AI systems (Claude, DeepSeek, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Mistral) that helped shape it are all still here — relocated to the [Accountability Framework](../../projects/accountability-framework/index.md) project. Nothing in the framework itself was loosened or rewritten in the move; the sourcing discipline, the three disqualifiers, the scope axiom all carry over unchanged. Only the address changed.

## Why

A DOD member raised a concern: the old setup had the relationship backwards. A long, AI-reviewed standard, gated by a "read this before touching it" soul document, reads as *settled*. That's a strange thing to hand someone as a front door. If you wanted to walk in and argue with DOD about democracy — disagree with a premise, raise an idea you suspected was unpopular, ask a half-formed question — the page told you, structurally, that the conversation was already finished.

That's what "monolithic" meant in the feedback: not that the framework was wrong, but that its placement was suppressing the free, repeat-yourself, say-the-unpopular-thing discussion the Philosophy section should exist for. The same member was clear that the rigor itself is not the problem — the sourcing discipline DOD applies to organisations and external claims is exactly as useful as it's always been. The fix wasn't to loosen that. It was to stop using it as the thing newcomers meet first.

## What's there now

The [Philosophy](../../philosophy/index.md) page is now a low-friction discussion space, open to any DOD member's idea about democracy, governance, or accountability — including ideas that didn't get traction before, ideas people suspect are unpopular, and disagreement with the Accountability Framework itself. Nothing posted there needs a citation.

It also carries a first draft of questions DOD wants to put to real people — online, and ideally at an in-person event — as a way of testing what this space should actually become, rather than deciding that in advance.

## What this isn't

This isn't a verdict that the framework was flawed, and it isn't a loosening of DOD's curation standards. The [Accountability Framework](../../projects/accountability-framework/index.md) does exactly what it did before, for exactly the same reason. It just no longer pretends to be a conversation starter.
