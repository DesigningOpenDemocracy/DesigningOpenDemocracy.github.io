International IDEA (the International Institute for Democracy and Electoral Assistance) is an intergovernmental organisation founded in 1995 with a mandate to support sustainable democratic governance across the globe. It has 35 member states and is headquartered in Stockholm, with regional offices across Africa, Asia-Pacific, Latin America, the Middle East, and Europe.

IDEA focuses on the full spectrum of democratic systems: electoral processes, political parties, constitutions, parliaments, and civic participation. It is distinct from advocacy organisations — its role is to provide comparative research and technical assistance to governments, electoral commissions, and civil society, without prescribing a single model of democracy.

Key resources and platforms:

- **Global State of Democracy** — annual indices tracking democratic performance across 160+ countries
- **ConstitutionNet** — an online platform for constitutional drafters and researchers, hosting global resources on constitution-building processes
- **Electoral Integrity** — comparative data on electoral system design and administration
- **Voter Turnout Database** — one of the most comprehensive global datasets on electoral participation

For organisations working on democratic reform or civic tech, IDEA's comparative databases are a primary research resource.
