> **Note:** The CPPCC operates under explicit CCP leadership as part of China's "united front" framework. It is not an independent institution — its advisory role is circumscribed by party discipline and all major directions originate from or require CCP approval. It is included here for comparative understanding of how political consultation is institutionalised within a single-party system, not as an independent democracy organisation.

The Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (中国人民政治协商会议) was established in 1949 and is China's top political advisory body. Its National Committee comprises approximately 3,000 members drawn from the CPC itself, the eight officially recognised "democratic parties," mass organisations, ethnic minority representatives, and unaffiliated delegates from sectors including science, education, culture, and business.

The CPPCC's official functions are framed as "political consultation, democratic supervision, and participation in state affairs." Regional committees extend to provincial, prefectural, and county levels. Membership rotates on five-year terms. The National Committee Chairperson is a member of the Politburo Standing Committee.

## Role in China's political system

The CPPCC is central to China's "multi-party cooperation and political consultation" system and to the official concept of **whole-process people's democracy** (全过程人民民主) — Xi Jinping's framework positioning Chinese governance as a model of continuous civic participation rather than periodic competitive elections. From a comparative governance perspective, the CPPCC illustrates how formalised consultation and multi-sector representation can be structured within a single-party state.
