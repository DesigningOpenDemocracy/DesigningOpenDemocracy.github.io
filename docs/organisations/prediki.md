Prediki (Prediki Prediction Markets GmbH) is a peer-to-peer platform for opinion research and collective intelligence, built around prediction market methodology. Rather than simple polling, participants make structured predictions about outcomes, stake a position, and provide reasons — the system then aggregates these into a single probability estimate while tracking the quality of each contributor's predictions over time.

The platform was developed by Hubertus Hofkirchner, who presented it at a [2017 Designing Open Democracy event](../../blog/posts/2017-08-21-podcast.md) alongside Nicolas Gruen. The DOD presentation framed Prediki specifically as a democratic tool: a way to move beyond vox-pop opinion polling toward *considered* collective intelligence.

## How it differs from polling

The core insight is that asking people to *predict* an outcome (and be accountable for that prediction) engages a different mode of reasoning than asking what they prefer or believe. Prediction markets have a strong empirical track record of outperforming conventional surveys on accuracy.

Prediki adds:

- **Reason capture** — participants specify why they are making a prediction, enabling sentiment and argument analysis
- **Reputation tracking** — contributors with better track records carry more weight
- **Argument mapping** — the system can break down opinion clusters and the reasons behind them

## Meine Stimme GILT

Hofkirchner is also an executive board member of **Meine Stimme GILT** ("My Voice Counts"), a non-partisan Austrian civic organisation focused on decentralising collective political decision-making. GILT represents the civic application of the ideas behind Prediki's commercial platform.
