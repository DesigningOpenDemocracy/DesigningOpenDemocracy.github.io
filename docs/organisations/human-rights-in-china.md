Human Rights in China (中国人权, HRIC) was founded in March 1989 by Chinese students and scholars in New York in the immediate aftermath of the Tiananmen Square protests. It operates from the United States as an international advocacy organisation focused specifically on China, and is one of the longest-standing diaspora organisations working on Chinese democracy and civil liberties.

HRIC's work includes case advocacy for detained activists and lawyers, policy analysis on legal and political conditions in mainland China and Hong Kong, and translation of key Chinese laws and judicial documents for international audiences.

## Note on scope

HRIC operates in diaspora because independent civil society of this kind cannot operate freely on the Chinese mainland. It is included here as part of the broader democracy landscape — documenting conditions and advocating for change from outside China's borders.
