Co-operative Bonds is a cooperative development consultancy structured as a cooperative itself. Founded by Antony McMullen, Clare Fountain, and Paul Saeki, it provides education and development services to help purpose-driven member-based organisations build financially viable and sustainable models.

McMullen presented the economic democracy case at a [2020 Designing Open Democracy podcast](../blog/posts/2020-06-20-podcast.md) on moving beyond Corporate Social Responsibility toward structurally democratic economic institutions.

## What they do

- Governance consulting and framework design
- Business model development and economic modelling
- Adult learning and cooperative education
- Corporate strategy for member-driven enterprises

Co-operative Bonds notably co-drafted the **Co-operative and Mutual Enterprise (CME) governance framework** for the Australian Business Council of Co-operatives and Mutuals (BCCM) in 2018 — a best-practice governance standard for the sector.

## Cooperatives they have supported

The consultancy has supported the development of several cooperative ventures, including:

- **[bHive](https://bhive.coop/)** (Bendigo) — Australia's first platform cooperative for neighbourhood resource and skill sharing
- **Cooperative Power Australia** — renewable energy cooperative
- **Earthworker Co-operative** — worker cooperative ecosystem including Redgum Cleaners Cooperative
- **ORICoop** — Organic and Regenerative Investment Cooperative

## About the founder

Antony McMullen is a Fellow of the Governance Institute of Australia (FGIA) and the Royal Society for the Encouragement of Arts, Manufactures and Commerce (FRSA). He also co-founded [888 Co-operative Causeway](888-cooperative-causeway.md) and the (now closed) Co-op Incubator.
