MASS LBP is a Toronto-based civic design firm founded in 2007 that specialises in designing and running citizens' assemblies, civic lotteries, and deliberative public engagement processes. It takes its name from the French phrase *masse de manœuvre* — a reserve force held back for decisive deployment.

MASS LBP has completed over 50 "reference panels" (their term for mini-publics) for governments, institutions, and organisations across Canada and the United States. Their work spans planning, policy, infrastructure, healthcare, and governance reform.

Key projects include:

- **Ontario Citizens' Reference Panel on Electoral Reform** (2007) — one of the first large-scale citizens' assemblies in Canada, recommending Mixed Member Proportional representation
- Multiple reference panels for the **City of Toronto** on infrastructure priorities and civic issues
- Work with **Infrastructure Ontario**, **Waterfront Toronto**, and other major public bodies
- **Better Transit Citizens' Reference Panel** — engaging Torontonians on transit priorities

MASS LBP's methodology draws heavily on sortition — random selection to create a representative cross-section of the public — combined with structured deliberation, expert testimony, and facilitated small-group discussion. Their practice emphasises the quality of the deliberative process over the quantity of participants.
