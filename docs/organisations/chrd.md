Chinese Human Rights Defenders (CHRD) is a coalition of Chinese and international organisations dedicated to promoting human rights, democratic reform, and rule of law in China through peaceful activism and documentation. It is headquartered in Washington DC and operates as a diaspora advocacy network.

CHRD monitors and documents systematic repression — particularly targeting prisoners of conscience, human rights lawyers, and peaceful dissidents — and engages with international policy processes including UN mechanisms.

## Note on scope

Like other China-focused democracy organisations, CHRD operates outside mainland China where independent civil society of this kind faces severe legal restrictions. It is included here as part of the democracy landscape covering Chinese civil society from diaspora.
